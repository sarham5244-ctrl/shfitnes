Cardio vs Weight Training: Which Should You Do?

By SH Fitness Tools Team Published 25 May 2026 6 min read

This doesn't have to be an either-or decision. Cardio and weight training do different jobs, and most people benefit from both.

On this page

  1. What each one does best
  2. If your priority is fat loss
  3. If your priority is general fitness

What each one does best

Weight training is the more effective tool for building and preserving muscle, and it's what creates the shape most people associate with an athletic physique. Cardio is the more effective tool for cardiovascular health and endurance, and it burns calories efficiently during the session itself, which makes it a useful lever for adding to a fat loss deficit without needing to eat drastically less.

If your priority is fat loss

Fat loss ultimately comes down to a calorie deficit, and weight training combined with adequate protein does the heavy lifting for preserving muscle while that deficit runs. Cardio is a genuinely useful addition on top of this — it burns extra calories and supports the deficit — but it works best as a complement to weight training, not a replacement for it, particularly if you don't want to lose hard-earned muscle alongside the fat.

If your priority is general fitness

For overall health, a mix of both is well supported — resistance training at least twice a week alongside some form of regular cardiovascular activity covers the bases that either one alone misses. Tools like the Heart Rate Zone Calculator and Calories Burned Calculator help structure the cardio side, while the One Rep Max Calculator helps structure the strength side.
