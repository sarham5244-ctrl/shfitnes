Creatine Explained: Benefits, Safety and How It Works

By SH Fitness Tools Team Published 23 February 2026 6 min read

Creatine is one of the most researched supplements in sports nutrition, and also one of the most misunderstood. Here's what it actually does.

On this page

  1. How creatine actually works
  2. What the research shows
  3. How to dose it properly

How creatine actually works

Creatine helps regenerate ATP, the molecule your muscles use for immediate energy during short, high-intensity efforts like a heavy lift or a sprint. Your body produces some creatine naturally and stores more in muscle from dietary sources like red meat, but supplementing raises those stores further, which is where the performance benefit comes from — slightly more available energy for those short, hard efforts, set after set.

What the research shows

Creatine monohydrate is consistently associated with small but real improvements in strength and power output, and it may modestly support muscle growth over time, mainly by allowing slightly harder training sessions. It's also linked to a small amount of water retention inside muscle cells, which is normal and part of how it works, not a sign of bloating or fat gain. Long-term studies, including some spanning years of continuous use, haven't found evidence of harm to kidney or liver function in healthy individuals at standard doses.

How to dose it properly

A daily maintenance dose of around 3-5g works well for most people, and taking it at any time of day is fine — consistency matters more than timing. Some people choose a short loading phase of about 0.3g per kg for 5-7 days to saturate muscle stores faster, but it's optional; skipping it just means reaching full saturation over 3-4 weeks instead of one.
