How Many Sets and Reps Should You Do?

By SH Fitness Tools Team Published 27 April 2026 6 min read

Sets and reps advice online ranges wildly depending on who you ask. Here's how to actually choose based on what you're training for.

On this page

  1. Rep ranges by goal
  2. Total volume matters more than the exact rep number
  3. A simple starting structure

Rep ranges by goal

Lower reps in the 1-5 range, with heavier weight and longer rest, tend to prioritise pure strength gains. Moderate reps around 6-12 are the classic hypertrophy range, generally offering a good balance of mechanical tension and enough volume to drive muscle growth. Higher reps above 12-15 lean more toward muscular endurance, though research increasingly shows this range can still build meaningful muscle when taken close to failure.

Total volume matters more than the exact rep number

Within a reasonably wide range, the total volume you accumulate — sets multiplied by reps multiplied by weight — tends to matter more for growth than hitting a specific rep number precisely. This means a rigid obsession with exactly 8-12 reps every set is less important than showing up consistently and doing enough total hard work across the week. Most well-designed programs land somewhere around 10-20 total working sets per muscle group per week for most trained lifters.

A simple starting structure

If you're not sure where to start, 3-4 sets of 6-12 reps for your main lifts, taken close to (but not always all the way to) failure, is a reliable general-purpose structure that works for most goals. Use the One Rep Max Calculator to find working percentages if you want to train in a more specific strength-focused rep range for a block of your program.
