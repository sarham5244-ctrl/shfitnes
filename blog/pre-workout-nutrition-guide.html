Pre-Workout Nutrition: What Should You Eat Before Training?

By SH Fitness Tools Team Published 9 February 2026 5 min read

What you eat before training affects how the session actually feels — here's how to time it without overthinking every session.

On this page

  1. What to prioritise before training
  2. Timing it right
  3. What if you train first thing in the morning?

What to prioritise before training

Carbohydrates are the priority pre-workout, since they top up muscle glycogen and provide readily available energy for the session ahead. A moderate amount of protein alongside it isn't essential for performance that day, but doesn't hurt and helps keep your daily protein target on track. Fat and fibre are worth keeping lower right before training, since both slow digestion and can leave you feeling heavy or sluggish once you're moving.

Timing it right

A larger meal 2-3 hours before training gives your body time to digest properly, while a smaller, faster-digesting snack 30-60 minutes out works well if you're training soon after eating is possible. Neither timing is objectively "correct" — it's about matching the meal size to how much time you actually have, and noticing what leaves you feeling best during the session itself.

What if you train first thing in the morning?

Training fasted works fine for many people, especially for lower-intensity sessions, and isn't something to force yourself out of if it feels fine. For harder sessions — heavy lifting or high-intensity intervals — even a small carb source beforehand, like a piece of fruit or a slice of toast, can noticeably improve how the session feels without needing a full meal. If you're unsure how many calories you're working with for the day overall, our Macro Calculator is a good starting point for building the rest of your meals around it.
